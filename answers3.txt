Question: 1. What was the primary motivation for leaders from 193 countries to come together in 2015 and create the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)?
Answer: The primary motivation for leaders from 193 countries to come together in 2015 and create the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) was to address daunting global challenges such as famines, drought, wars, plagues, and poverty. They recognized that these issues were not confined to distant places but were affecting their own cities, towns, and villages. They also acknowledged that there were solutions to these problems, such as sharing food more equitably, making medicines more affordable, and improving disaster preparedness. Additionally, they were motivated by the shared hope of billions of people worldwide for a better future.
----------
Question: 2. What are the three main issues mentioned that the leaders knew could be addressed to improve the world's conditions?
Answer: The three main issues mentioned that leaders knew could be addressed to improve the world's conditions are:

1. **Promoting policies that encourage entrepreneurship and job creation.**
2. **Eradicating forced labour, slavery, and human trafficking.**
3. **Achieving the goal of decent work for all women and men by 2030.**
----------
Question: 3. What is the primary objective of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) by the year 2030?
Answer: The primary objective of the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) by the year 2030 is to create a future that is free from poverty and hunger, and safe from the worst effects of climate change. This ambitious plan aims to address global challenges such as extreme poverty, hunger, inequality, and environmental degradation.
----------
Question: 4. How many countries and territories does the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) operate in to help fulfill the SDGs?
Answer: The United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) operates in nearly 170 countries and territories to help fulfill the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs).
----------
Question: 5. What specific goal does the UNDP aim to achieve by 2030 regarding extreme poverty, and what was the significant milestone achieved in 2000?
Answer: The UNDP aims to end extreme poverty in all forms by 2030. The significant milestone achieved in 2000 was the world's commitment to halving the number of people living in extreme poverty by the year 2015, which was successfully met.
